Neil Armstrong (United States)

Neil Armstrong was an American astronaut, aeronautical engineer, and naval aviator. He made history on July 20, 1969, during the Apollo 11 mission, when he became the first person to walk on the Moon, famously stating, “That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind.” Before becoming an astronaut, he served as a test pilot and flew over 200 different aircraft. After retiring from NASA, Armstrong became a university professor and continued to advocate for space exploration.

Chris Hadfield

Chris Hadfield is a retired Canadian astronaut, engineer, and former Royal Canadian Air Force fighter pilot. He gained international fame during his time aboard the ISS in 2013, where he conducted scientific experiments and shared life in space through social media. He also recorded a viral cover of David Bowie's "Space Oddity" in orbit. Hadfield has flown two Space Shuttle missions and served as the commander of Expedition 35. After retiring, he became a public speaker and author, inspiring many with his insights on space and leadership.

Valentina Tereshkova (Russia)

Valentina Tereshkova was a Soviet cosmonaut and engineer. On June 16, 1963, she launched aboard Vostok 6, becoming the first woman to travel to space. Her mission lasted nearly three days, during which she orbited Earth 48 times. Before her spaceflight, she was a textile factory worker and amateur skydiver. Her flight was a major milestone in the space race and for women's representation in science and exploration. She later became a politician and remains a celebrated figure in Russian history.
